What is your personal working definition of “design”? (By “working” I mean for you currently and it is okay if this definition is aspirational.) You can reference the articles, and you can bring your own references if desired.

The readings this week reminded me of Ellen Lupton’s 1998 essay “The Designer as Producer,” which I think is closer to where my personal definition of design stands. I think it’s important, first and foremost, to differentiate between design and fine art. Design has a component of utility that ties it to its consequences in the real world in a much more tangible and direct way.

While I agree with Michael Rock’s argument that form has value in its own right, his essay treads dangerously close to self-indulgence. His comparison of design to filmmaking, this idea of design about design, for design’s sake, places design in an elitist bubble cut off from the wider socio-political context. Especially in our current political climate, dialogues surrounding ethics, responsibility, and complicity are vital for the future of graphic design and design in general.

I much more advocate the points Daniel van der Velden makes in his essay, but also understand that most working designers do not have the privilege of exiting the client/commission system. Therefore, I come back to “The Designer as Producer,” where the designer directs content through considered form, “by critically navigating the social, aesthetic, and technological systems across which communications flow.”
